RESEARCH INTERESTS

Pre-2020 (Harvard Period): Environmental and Cardiovascular Epidemiology

During nine years at Harvard University (2011–2020), my research focused on environmental epidemiology using the Framingham Heart Study (FHS). Focusing on ambient air pollution, traffic-related emissions, meteorological factors, and tobacco smoke, I systematically investigated their multi-system health effects and underlying molecular and imaging mechanisms. As first author, I finished multiple original cohort studies; as co-author, participated in high-impact collaborative projects. Total citation of my published papers exceeds 400.

Post-2020 (Zhejiang University Period): AI-Driven Aging Assessment and Multi-Modal Health Data Science

Four core research directions: aging research, environmental epidemiology, AI-driven health prediction modeling, interdisciplinary collaborative research. Leveraging Chinese population cohorts, NHANES, UK Biobank and other international databases, integrating deep learning, machine learning, multi-omics and prospective cohort design to complete translational public health research, serving as corresponding author for a series of original studies.

Major Cohort Platform Development

Deputy Director of the Joint Office of the Healthy Zhejiang One Million People Cohort. Participated in overall framework design and on-site construction, built three-tier (provincial–prefectural–field) standardized quality control system. Over 800,000 participants enrolled as of May 2026. Integrate genomics, medical imaging, clinical records, environmental and lifestyle data to explore gene-environment interaction in human aging.
